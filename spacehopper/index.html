Abstract

We introduce SpaceHopper, an innovative small-scale, three-legged robot designed for the exploration of low-gravity celestial bodies. SpaceHopper uniquely employs jumping locomotion as its primary mode of movement. Distinctively, it utilizes its legs, rather than a conventional reaction wheel, to control its attitude during flight phases. The robot harnesses Deep Reinforcement Learning to adeptly manage the highly dynamic jumping and attitude control maneuvers. We conducted comprehensive testing of the entire system in zero-gravity conditions during a parabolic flight campaign in collaboration with the European Space Agency. These tests successfully demonstrated SpaceHopper's abilities to reorient to random attitudes using its legs and to jump to a commanded velocity. This research marks a significant advancement in robotic mobility for space exploration, offering promising applications in future extraterrestrial missions.
